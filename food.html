Eating for a Healthy Heart

Learn about heart-healthy foods and simple recipes for a stronger heart

A healthy heart starts with healthy eating. The foods you choose every day can have a big impact on your heart’s strength and your risk of heart disease. By making small changes to your diet, you can protect your heart and enjoy delicious meals at the same time.

Heart-Healthy Foods

  • Fruits and Vegetables: Eat a variety of colors—leafy greens, berries, oranges, tomatoes, and carrots are all great choices.
  • Whole Grains: Choose oats, brown rice, whole wheat bread, and quinoa over refined grains.
  • Healthy Fats: Use olive oil, nuts, seeds, and avocados instead of butter or lard. Include fatty fish like salmon or mackerel twice a week.
  • Lean Proteins: Opt for skinless poultry, beans, lentils, and tofu.
  • Low-Fat Dairy: Select low-fat or non-fat milk, yogurt, and cheese.
  • Limit Salt and Sugar: Reduce processed foods, sugary drinks, and salty snacks.

Simple Heart-Healthy Recipes

Oatmeal with Berries and Nuts
Cook 1/2 cup oats with 1 cup skim milk or water. Top with a handful of fresh berries and a tablespoon of chopped walnuts or almonds. Sprinkle with cinnamon for extra flavor.
Grilled Salmon Salad
Grill a salmon fillet with lemon juice and olive oil. Serve on a bed of spinach, cherry tomatoes, cucumbers, and red onion. Drizzle with a homemade vinaigrette of olive oil and lemon.
Veggie Stir-Fry with Tofu
Sauté broccoli, bell peppers, and carrots in olive oil. Add cubed tofu or chicken breast. Season with garlic, ginger, and a splash of low-sodium soy sauce. Serve over brown rice or quinoa.
Berry Yogurt Parfait
Layer low-fat Greek yogurt with sliced strawberries, blueberries, and a sprinkle of chia seeds or flaxseeds. Top with a few chopped nuts for crunch.
Spinach & Chickpea Salad (Indian)
Toss baby spinach, boiled chickpeas, diced cucumber, cherry tomatoes, and red onion. Dress with olive oil, lemon juice, and black pepper. Garnish with coriander.
Barley and Moong Dal Khichdi (Indian)
... Cook 1/2 cup barley and 1/2 cup yellow moong dal with chopped carrots, beans, and spinach. Temper with cumin, ginger, and garlic in a teaspoon of olive oil. Serve hot with a side of low-fat curd.
Rich in fiber and protein, low in fat and sodium.
Oats Vegetable Upma (Indian)
Dry roast 1/2 cup rolled oats. Sauté onions, carrots, beans, and peas in a teaspoon of oil, add oats and water, season with mustard seeds, curry leaves, and turmeric. Cook till soft. Serve with fresh coriander.
High in soluble fiber, helps lower cholesterol.
Sprouted Moong Salad (Indian)
Mix sprouted moong, chopped tomatoes, cucumber, onion, and coriander. Add lemon juice, black pepper, and a pinch of chaat masala. Enjoy as a crunchy, protein-rich snack or side.
Lentil & Vegetable Soup
Cook chopped onions, carrots, celery, and garlic in olive oil. Add rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, and low-sodium vegetable broth. Simmer until lentils are soft. Season with herbs like thyme and parsley.
Low-Fat Chaas (Indian Buttermilk)
Blend low-fat curd with water, add roasted cumin powder, chopped coriander, mint, and a pinch of salt. Serve chilled as a refreshing drink.

Tips for Heart-Healthy Eating

  • Cook more meals at home to control ingredients and portion sizes.
  • Read nutrition labels and choose foods low in saturated fat, sodium, and added sugars.
  • Stay hydrated—drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Enjoy treats in moderation and focus on balance, not perfection.

Small, consistent changes can make a big difference for your heart. Enjoy exploring new foods and recipes as you build a stronger, healthier you!
