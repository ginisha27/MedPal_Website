Health Screening Checklist

Health Screening as a Tool

Regular health screenings are powerful tools for early detection and prevention of diseases. By following a screening checklist, you can catch potential health issues before they become serious, ensuring timely treatment and better outcomes. Screenings are essential for maintaining long-term health and peace of mind.

  • Blood Pressure: Check at least once every 1-2 years for adults.
  • Cholesterol: Screen every 4-6 years, or as recommended.
  • Blood Glucose: Test for diabetes risk, especially if overweight or family history exists.
  • Cancer Screenings: Mammograms, Pap smears, colonoscopies, and prostate checks as age/gender appropriate.
  • Vision & Hearing: Regular checks, especially after age 40.
  • Immunizations: Stay updated on vaccines (flu, tetanus, COVID-19, etc.).
