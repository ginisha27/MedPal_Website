Understanding Type 2 Diabetes

Find out about the causes, symptoms, and management of type 2 diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is a chronic condition that affects the way your body metabolizes sugar (glucose), an important source of fuel for your body. Unlike type 1 diabetes, where the body produces little or no insulin, people with type 2 diabetes develop insulin resistance, meaning their bodies do not use insulin effectively.

Causes

  • Insulin resistance: The body's cells become less responsive to insulin.
  • Genetics: Family history increases risk.
  • Overweight and inactivity: Excess weight, especially around the abdomen, and a sedentary lifestyle are major risk factors.
  • Age: Risk increases after age 45, but type 2 diabetes is rising among younger people.
  • Other factors: Ethnicity, high blood pressure, and poor diet can also contribute.

Symptoms

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Fatigue and weakness
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
  • Tingling or numbness in hands or feet
  • Darkened skin in certain areas (such as neck or armpits)

Management

  • Healthy eating: Focus on whole grains, fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Limit processed foods and sugary drinks.
  • Regular physical activity: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate exercise per week, such as brisk walking or cycling.
  • Weight management: Losing even a small amount of weight can improve blood sugar control.
  • Monitoring blood sugar: Regularly check your blood glucose levels as advised by your healthcare provider.
  • Medications: Some people may need oral medications or insulin therapy to help control their diabetes.
  • Routine checkups: Regular visits to your healthcare team help monitor your condition and prevent complications.

Early detection and proper management of type 2 diabetes can help you lead a healthy and active life. If you have risk factors or notice symptoms, consult your healthcare provider for screening and guidance.
